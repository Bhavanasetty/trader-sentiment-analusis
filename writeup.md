### Trader Behavior vs Market Sentiment Analysis

**Methodology:**
This analysis explores the relationship between Bitcoin market sentiment (Fear vs Greed) and trader behavior using Hyperliquid trading data. The datasets were first cleaned and aligned on a daily level by converting timestamps into date format. Missing values and inconsistencies were handled to ensure data quality. Key features such as daily profit and loss (PnL), trade frequency, average position size, and buy/sell ratios were engineered to capture trader behavior effectively.

**Key Findings:**
The analysis reveals that market sentiment significantly influences trader performance and behavior. During Greed periods, traders tend to achieve higher average profitability, but this is accompanied by increased volatility, indicating a higher risk-reward environment. In contrast, Fear periods show reduced trading activity but more inconsistent outcomes.

Behaviorally, traders execute more trades and take larger positions during Greed, suggesting confidence-driven decision-making. However, during Fear periods, frequent traders tend to underperform, likely due to overtrading in uncertain and volatile conditions.

**Behavioral Insights:**
The findings suggest that trader psychology plays a critical role in performance. Greed leads to aggressive participation and risk-taking, while Fear results in cautious yet sometimes irrational behavior. This highlights the importance of aligning trading strategies with prevailing market sentiment.

**Strategy Recommendations:**

1. During Fear periods, traders should reduce trade frequency and position sizes to manage risk effectively and avoid unnecessary exposure to volatility.
2. During Greed periods, traders can increase participation and position sizes gradually but should implement strict risk management techniques such as stop-loss mechanisms to prevent large drawdowns.

**Conclusion:**
Market sentiment is a key driver of both trader behavior and performance. By incorporating sentiment-aware strategies, traders can improve decision-making and achieve better risk-adjusted returns. This analysis demonstrates that combining behavioral insights with market indicators can lead to more effective trading strategies.
